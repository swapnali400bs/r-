Special Auditing for Checking Fraudulent Activities

Apart from the general audit government performs on an organization, this emphasizes on detecting fraud schemes and provide prevention controls to help mitigate fraud risk. Following are the common fraud schemes:
  • Skimming: Money intended for the government that an individual takes for personal use.
  • Forgery or alterations: Includes checks, p-cards, vendor invoices, or employee payroll that are forged or altered.
  • Unauthorized vendor distributions: Payments may be made to a fictitious vendor for goods never received or a legitimate vendor for personal goods.
  • Unauthorized payroll disbursements: This can include fictitious employees, unauthorized pay increases, or overtime.
